Early Bowie (1960s)

David Robert Jones was born on 8 January 1947, in Brixton, south London, England. His mother, Margaret Mary "Peggy" was born in Kent, and had Irish ancestry; she worked as a waitress. His father, Haywood Stenton "John" Jones (1912-1969), from Yorkshire, was a promotions officer for the childrens charity Barnardo's. The family lived at 40 Stansfield Road, near the border of the south London areas of Brixton and Stockwell.

Rebel, Rebel - 1974

A glam-rock anthem if there ever was one, "Rebel, Rebel" features one of the most recognizable guitar riffs in rock history - and it was Bowie himself who handled lead guitar duties on this track.

Bowie's most famous screen appearance was in the 1986 Jim Henson film Labyrinth in which Bowie played Jareth the Goblin King. With the exeption of Bowie and actress Jennifer Connelly, the majority of the characters are puppets created by Jim Henson. Although the movie did not do well at its initial release, it has gained a cult following over the years.

the labyrinth

The characters of Jim Henson's cult classic film.

jareth

Jareth is the Goblin King and ruler over the Labyrinth. He challenges Sarah to navigate the Labyrinth to rescue her baby brother.

hoggle

Hoggle is a supporting character in Labyrinth, and becomes a loyal friend of Sarah's after he defies his master Jareth to accompany her on her quest.

ludo

Ludo is a gigantic beast who Sarah rescues from a group of cruel goblins.

the worm

The worm is extremely hospitable, and offers Sarah guidance when she is at the point of giving up her quest to save her brother. He has a cockney accent and wears a bright red scarf.

sir didymus

Sir Didymus is an often illogically heroic knight who Sarah encounters when she attempts to leave the Bog Of Eternal Stench.

sarah williams

Sarah is the main protaganist of the film and travels through the Labyrynth to rescue her baby brother who she unknowingly wished away to Jareth the Goblin King.
