Early Bowie (1960s)

David Robert Jones was born on 8 January 1947, in Brixton, south London, England. His mother, Margaret Mary "Peggy" was born in Kent, and had Irish ancestry; she worked as a waitress. His father, Haywood Stenton "John" Jones (1912-1969), from Yorkshire, was a promotions officer for the childrens charity Barnardo's. The family lived at 40 Stansfield Road, near the border of the south London areas of Brixton and Stockwell.

David Bowie is the debut studio album released on 1 June 1967, on Deram Records. Its content bears little overt resemblance to the type of music that later made him famous.

Dressed in a striking costume, his hair dyed reddish-brown, Bowie launched his Ziggy Stardust stage show with the Spiders from Mars-Ronson, Bolder and Woodmansey-at the Toby Jug pub in Tolworth on 10 February 1972. Its influence lasted longer and has been more creative than perhaps almost any other force within pop fandom.
